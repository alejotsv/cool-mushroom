Learn about chaga

Benefits

Chaga is high in Beta Glucans, which may promote white blood cell activation and catalyze the growth of healthy new skin cells. This is especially true when Chaga is used topically, for example in a lotion or soap.

Value

The antioxidant concentration of Chaga is statistically unrivaled among naturally occuring foods. It’s 45x higher than acai berries, 80x higher than pomegranates, and 1,300x higher than blueberries. Almost 1,300 times that of blueberries!

Where they come from

Chaga is a fungus belonging to the genus Inonotus. In Latin, Inonotus means “to penetrate”, which reflects the reproductive nature of Chaga’s spores, finding their way into a wound or crevice on their host tree. In appearance, Chaga looks like a black growth protruding from the side of a tree's trunk.
